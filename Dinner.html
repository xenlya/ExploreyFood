1. Chinese Dinner: Chow Mein

Description

It all comes down to the sauce, which is composed, quite simply, of soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar and cornstarch to thicken. Slick noodles coated in that savoury sauce is noodle nirvana! The only thing which sets Chow Mein apart from other stir fried noodles is the type of noodles. Chow Mein noodles are fine crinkly looking noodles with a light dusting of flour.

2. Indian Dinner: Methi Pakoda Kadhi

Description

The Methi Pakoda Kadhi Recipe is a lip smacking Indian Kadhi recipe where the traditional punjabi kadhi is simmered along with Methi pakoras. The kadhi is further tempered with some ghee to enhance the scrumptious taste. We have prepared a crunchy methi pakoda with the help of a Paniyaram pans, so its healthy as it requires less oil for frying. The methi pakoras are then dropped into the hot kadhi cooked until soft.

3. Korean Dinner: Bibimbap

Description

Although we stir them together before we've taken a bite, the elements of bibimbap must be made with distinct care and character in mind, to bring out the unique flavors, textures and colors so they meld into a gorgeous whole in the bowl and a tasty whole in your mouth. These various ingredients aren't random, but selected because they balance, harmonize and offset one another.

4. Japanese Dinner: Beef Sukiyaki

Description

Beef sukiyaki is a delicious Japanese dish that combines vegetables, noodles, tofu, and steak in a steaming, flavorful broth made with dashi, mirin, and soy sauce. Traditionally cooked at the table in a simmering pot, this recipe offers the convenience of preparing it on the stovetop instead.
