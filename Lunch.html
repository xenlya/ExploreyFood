Chinese Lunch: Wonton Soup

Description

Wonton soup is a classic Chinese soup of chicken broth and wontons seasoned with seasonings. This consists of a pork and shrimp wonton seasoned with Shaoxing wine, ginger, shallots, soy sauce, sesame oil, and Shaoxing wine. It is also usually served with dark leafy greens such as blanched bok choy. This straightforward wonton soup is a great dinner or lunch on its own, but you can always accompany it with other meals like shrimp fried rice or mapo tofu and rice.

Indian Lunch: Mutton Biryani

Description

Before we proceed to the specific charm of Mutton Biryani, let us take a moment to consider the rich heritage of biryani itself. Its Indian subcontinent origins imbue it with the whispers of history, the mixing of cultures, and the creativity of generations past. Biryani, in all its forms, has been enjoyed across lands, each version a unique tale of local ingredients, techniques, and the skill of master chefs.

Korean Lunch: Tteokbokki

Description

Wondering how to say tteokbokki? Just say it with us: "duck-bok-kee." Built on gloriously chewy rice cakes and pan-Asian flavors, this Korean treat rolls right off your tongue. Stock your pantry with gochujang! Like kimchi, this dark red hot sauce is a Korean culinary workhorse. Formulated from chile powder, glutinous rice, fermented soybeans, and salt, it adds a burst of sweetness, umami, and medium heat to anything it touches.

Japanese Lunch: Yakisoba

Description

Yakisoba is a classic Japanese noodle dish with salty, sweet and sour sauce. One-pan cooked, it's so simple to make and is always a winner. Yakisoba noodles are similar to ramen noodles but not exactly the same. They're made of wheat and should be stir-fried. An original yakisoba is made using finely sliced pork, carrots, cabbage, onions and a distinctive sweet and salty dressing. We like to use chicken as our choice of yakisoba, but you can substitute with pork if you prefer!
